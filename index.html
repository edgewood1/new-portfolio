Who Am I?



Click for Resume


As a teenager, I explored some basic forms of coding. This, in a roundabout way, led me into an Ohio engineering program, which I briefly attended before switching into literature and philosophy, both of which I still have a deep affinity for.

Later, I taught writing and research classes at various NC colleges, exploring the ways that we inform and are informed by society and media.

Curious about other forms of relationship, I studied and worked as an outdoor guide at an eco-contemplative center, and I facilitated sales, operations, and communications at a wildcrafting & outdoor school.

Presently, I've returned to writing code, exploring software / web development. I appreciate the way coding integrates many of my past interests while allowing for new forms of expression.

I enjoy the elements and grammar of computer languages as well as the kinds of applications they can create and the kinds of community and collaboration that they can foster.

I also enjoy the ways that a front-end design can connect with a user, the ways its data can pass back and forth among various databases, and the restraints that security protocols can place on this passage.
