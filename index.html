Who Am I?

As a youngster, I had a fondness for an early form of the BASIC computer language.

In a roundabout way, this led me into an Ohio engineering program, which I briefly attended before switching into literature and philosophy, both of which I still have a deep affinity for.

Later, I taught writing and research classes at various NC colleges, exploring the ways that we inform and are informed by society and media.

Curious about other forms of relationship, I studied and worked as an outdoor guide at an eco-contemplative center, and I facilitated sales, operational, and communications at a wildcrafting & outdoor school.

Presently, I've returned to writing code, exploring software / web development. I appreciate the way coding integrates many of my past interests while allowing for new forms of expression.
